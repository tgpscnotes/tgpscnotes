PAPER-I: GENERAL ESSAY

Each Essay carries 50 marks

Instructions: Candidate should write three Essays, selecting one from each Section compulsorily. Each Section contains three Questions.

Section-I

  • Contemporary Social Issues and Social Problems.
  • Issues of Economic Growth and Justice.

Section-II

  • Dynamics of Indian Politics.
  • Historical and Cultural Heritage of India.

Section-III

  • Developments in Science and Technology.
  • Education and Human Resource Development.

Essay Writing Strategy

  • Structure: Introduction, Body (3-4 paragraphs), Conclusion
  • Word Limit: Approximately 1000-1200 words per essay
  • Time Management: 1 hour per essay (Total 3 hours)
  • Use relevant examples, data, and current affairs
  • Maintain logical flow and coherence
  • Focus on original thought and analytical depth

PAPER-II: HISTORY, CULTURE AND GEOGRAPHY

History and Geography of India and Telangana
3 Hours 150 Marks

I. History and Culture of India (1757 to 1947 A.D.)

  1. Early Indian Civilizations: Indus and Vedic; Religious Movements: Jainism and Buddhism (6th century BC); Art & Architecture: Gandhara, Mathura and Amaravathi Schools; Social & Cultural conditions: Mauryan, Satavahanas and Guptas.
  2. Advent of Islam: Impact on Indian Society; Bhakti and Sufi Movements: Nature and significance; Contributions: Kakatiya, Vijayanagara, Delhi Sultans, Mughals; Composite Culture: Emergence in Deccan and India.
  3. British Colonial Rule: Carnatic Wars, Battle of Plassey, Anglo-Mysore, Anglo-Maratha, Anglo-Sikh Wars; Economic Impact: Land Revenue Settlements, Commercialization of Agriculture, Rise of Landless Labour, Famines, Poverty, De-industrialization, Drain of Wealth; Infrastructure: Railroads, Transport, Telegraph, Postal Services.
  4. Anti-British Uprisings: Tribal and Peasant revolts (19th Century); 1857 Revolt: Causes and Consequences; Indian Nationalism: Rise and factors; Reform Movements: Brahma Samaj, Arya Samaj, Aligarh Movement, Satya Shodak Samaj, Phule, Ramabai, Narayana Guru, Ayyankali, Annie Besant, Non-Brahmin, Justice, Self-Respect Movements.
  5. Freedom Struggle: Three Phases (1885-1947); Mass Movements: Kisan Sabha, Workers, Tribal movements; Women's Movement: Gender issues; Political Movements: Socialist, Communist, Communalism; Independence and Partition.

II. History and Cultural Heritage of Telangana

  1. Ancient Telangana: Satavahanas, Ikshvakus, Vishnukundins; Religions: Jainism and Buddhism; Socio-Cultural Conditions: Language, Literature, Art, Architecture.
  2. Medieval Telangana: Kakatiyas, Velama kingdoms; Composite Culture: Qutub Shahis contribution to Telugu literature, art, architecture; Protest Movements: Sammakka Sarakka, Sarvaipapanna.
  3. Asaf Jahi Dynasty: Salar Jung Reforms, Modernization; Socio-Economic Development: Land Tenures, Jagirdars, Deshmukhs, Vetti; British Paramountcy: Revolt of 1857 (Turre Baz Khan); Infrastructure: Railways, Transport, Communication, Industries, Education, Monuments.
  4. Socio-Cultural Awakening: Andhra Sarasvath Parishat, Literary and Library Movements; Political Organizations: Nizam Rashtra Andhra Jana Sangham, Andhra Mahasabha; Reform Movements: Brahma Samaj, Arya Samaj, Adi-Hindu, Dalit Movements (Bhagyareddy Varma); Women's Movement: Andhra Mahila Sabha; Political Movements: Hyderabad State Congress, Vandemataram Movement.
  5. People's Movement: Adivasi revolts (Ramji Gond, Kumaram Bheemu); Telangana Peasants Armed Struggle: Role of Andhra Mahasabha, Communists; Opposition: Majlis-Ittehadul-Muslimeen, Rajakars, Kasim Razvi; Integration: Police Action, End of Nizam's Rule, Hyderabad State into Indian Union.

III. Geography of India and Telangana

  1. India - Physical Geography: Physiography, Drainage, Climate; Monsoon: Mechanism, El-Nino, La Niña effects; Rainfall: Variability, Floods, Drought; Natural Resources: Soils, Vegetation, Wildlife, Minerals, Energy; Conservation: Degradation and measures; Marine Resources: EEZ; Water Resources: Availability, Inter-state sharing, Conservation.
  2. Agriculture & Irrigation: Major crops, Agro-climatic Regions, Green Revolution, Irrigation Projects; Industries: Iron & Steel, Cotton, Cement, Sugar, Automobile, IT, Food Processing; Transportation: Road, Rail, Ports, Highways; Trade: Trends, WTO role; Strategic Location: Indian Ocean; Population: Distribution, Growth, Demographics, HDI, Policies; Urbanization: Megacities, Smart Cities.
  3. Telangana Geography: Physical setting, Relief, Climate, Rivers, Soils; Forest & Wildlife: Distribution, depletion, conservation; Mineral Resources: Coal, Iron, Limestone; Power Projects: Thermal, Hydro.
  4. Agriculture: Rainfed/Dryland, Drought mitigation; Irrigation: Canals, Tanks, Wells, Groundwater conservation (Mission Kakatiya); Industries: Cement, Sugar, Pharma, Electronic, Tourism, IT, ITIR, SEZs; Handicrafts: Problems; Transport: Road and Rail network; Population: Distribution, density, demographics, Tribal areas development.
  5. Urbanization in Telangana: Spatio-temporal changes, Migration; Hyderabad: Evolution, transformation to Megapolis, City structure, Industries, Infrastructure (ORR, Metro); Planning: GHMC, HUDA (Metropolitan Development Plan 2031, HMDA); Tourism: Hyderabad as Tourist Centre and Global city.
Key Focus Areas for Paper-II
  • Modern Indian History (1757-1947) - British rule, nationalism, freedom struggle
  • Telangana History - Ancient to modern, cultural heritage, movement
  • Geography - Both Indian and Telangana-specific physical and human geography
  • Integration - Historical events leading to Telangana's formation
  • Current Relevance - Connect historical events with present-day Telangana

PAPER-III: INDIAN SOCIETY, CONSTITUTION AND GOVERNANCE

Society, Constitution and Governance
3 Hours 150 Marks Descriptive Type

I. Indian Society, Structure, Issues and Social Movements

  1. Indian Society: Salient features, Unity in Diversity; Social Institutions: Family, Marriage, Kinship, Caste, Tribe, Religion, Language; Social Continuum: Rural-Urban continuum; Multi-culturalism.
  2. Social Exclusion and Vulnerable Groups: Scheduled Castes, Scheduled Tribes, Backward Classes, Minorities, Women, Children, Aged and Disabled.
  3. Social Issues: Poverty; Unemployment, Child Labour, Violence against Women; Regionalism; Communalism and Secularism; Corruption; Caste Conflicts, Problems of Agricultural Labour; Urbanization; Development and Displacement; Environmental Degradation; Sustainable Development; Population Explosion; Agrarian Distress; Migration.
  4. (a) Social Issues in Telangana: Vetti; Jogini and Devadasi System; Girl Child; Fluorosis (Corrected spelling); Child Labour; Migrant Labour; Child Marriages.
    (b) Social Movements in Telangana.
  5. Social Policies and Programmes:
    India and Telangana: Policies for Women, Children, Aged and Disabled; Policies for Scheduled Castes, Scheduled Tribes, Backward Classes and Minorities.
    Sectoral Policies: Environmental Policy; Population Policy; Education Policy; Health Policy.
    Welfare Schemes: Poverty Alleviation Programmes; Welfare Schemes for SCs, STs, BCs, Women, Children, Minorities, Aged and Disabled.

II. Constitution of India

  1. Evolution: Role of the Drafting Committee; Constitutional Philosophy: Preamble; Salient features & Basic Structure; Amendments.
  2. Fundamental Rights: Nature and Scope; Expanding horizons; Enforceability against State and others; Directive Principles: Welfare State and Distributive Justice; Rule of Law and Fundamental Duties.
  3. System of Government: Parliamentary system; Central Government: President, Prime Minister & Council of Ministers; Parliament: Powers and Functions; State Government: Governor, Chief Minister & Council of Ministers; Legislature: Powers and Functions, Legislative Privileges.
  4. Judicial System: Supreme Court, High Courts & Administrative Tribunals; Subordinate Judiciary; Judicial Review and Activism: Independence of Judiciary and Judicial Accountability.
  5. Federal System: Centre-State Relations - Issues and Challenges; Local Self-Government: 73rd and 74th Constitutional Amendments (Panchayat Raj and Municipal Institutions); Inter-State Disputes: Water Disputes - Challenges of Implementation.

III. Governance

  1. Concepts: Governance and Good Governance, E-Governance (Applications and Models); Union Level Governance: Cabinet Secretariat, PMO, Central Secretariat, Ministries and Departments; Constitutional Bodies: Finance Commission, Election Commission, UPSC, CAG, NHRC, Commissions for SCs, STs, Minorities and Women; Parliamentary Committees: Estimates Committee, Public Accounts Committee, Committee on Public Undertakings.
  2. State and District Governance: Secretariat and Directorates; District Administration: Role of Collector; Rural and Urban Governance: Powers and Functions, Service Delivery Systems; Cooperatives, State Finance Commission; Devolution Issues: Powers and Finances; Development Corporations: SCs, STs, BCs, Minorities and Disabled Welfare; Administrative Control: Legislative, Executive and Judicial Control.
  3. Development Programmes: Urban and Rural development agencies; Participatory Development: People Centred approach; Poverty Alleviation: Programmes; Empowerment: Women Empowerment and Inclusive Growth; Rights Issues: Health, Food Security and Education - Issues and Challenges.
  4. Development Debates: Development Processes; State vs Market; Civil Society Involvement: CBOs, NGOs, SHGs, Charities, Stakeholders; Partnership Models: Public-Private Partnerships (PPP); Corporate Social Responsibility.
  5. Administrative Ethics: Ethics and Values; Neutrality of Civil Services; Politician-Civil Servant Relations; Transparency Tools: Citizen Charters, Gender Sensitization; Accountability Mechanisms: Prevention of Corruption - CVC, CBI, Lokpal, Lokayukta (Corrected spelling), ACB, Consumer Protection; Right to Information: RTI Act 2005 - Application and Impact; Administrative Reforms.
