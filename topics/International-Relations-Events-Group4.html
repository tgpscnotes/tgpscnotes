Part 1 — Foundations of International Relations

  • International Relations — Study of relationships between countries, including roles of states, intergovernmental organizations (IGOs), non-governmental organizations (NGOs), and multinational corporations (MNCs).
  • Nation State — A sovereign state whose citizens share common culture, history, and language.
  • Sovereignty — Supreme authority of a state over its territory and population.
  • National Interest — Goals and ambitions of a state (security, economic growth, influence).
  • Powers in IR — Hard power (military, economic) vs. Soft power (culture, diplomacy).
  • Diplomacy — Art and practice of conducting negotiations between nations.

Key Theories

  • Realism — States are main actors, power is central, international system is anarchic.
  • Liberalism — Emphasizes cooperation, international institutions, and interdependence.
  • Constructivism — Focuses on ideas, identities, and social constructs shaping IR.

Part 2 — Major Global Organizations

United Nations (UN)

  • Established — 1945
  • Purpose — Maintain international peace and security, promote human rights, foster development.
  • General Assembly — Deliberative assembly of all member states. o
  • Security Council — 15 members (5 permanent with veto – USA, UK, Russia, China, France).
  • International Court of Justice(ICJ) — judicial organ
  • Secretariat — Administrative organ headed by the Secretary-General.
  • Specialized Agencies — WHO, UNESCO, IMF, World Bank, ILO, FAO.
  • India’s Role — Founding member, major contributor to peacekeeping, advocates for UNSC reform.

G20

  • Members:19 countries + EU
  • Purpose: Forum for international economic cooperation.
  • India’s Presidency: 2023 – Theme: “Vasudhaiva Kutumbakam” – One Earth, One Family, One Future. Key outcomes: Green Development Pact, inclusion of African Union.

BRICS

  • Members: Brazil, Russia, India, China, South Africa + new members (2024): Egypt, Ethiopia, Iran, Saudi Arabia, UAE.
  • Purpose: Enhance cooperation among emerging economies, challenge Western dominance.
  • Initiatives: New Development Bank (NDB), Contingent Reserve Arrangement (CRA).

Part 3: India’s Foreign Policy

1. Principles & Evolution

  • Panchsheel (1954): Five principles of peaceful coexistence.
  • Non-Aligned Movement (NAM): During Cold War, not joining any power bloc.
  • Gujral Doctrine (1996): Unilateral concessions to neighbors for better relations.
  • Neighbourhood First Policy: Priority to relations with immediate neighbors.
  • Act East Policy (2014): Enhanced engagement with Southeast Asia and East Asia.
  • Think West Policy: Engagement with West Asia (Gulf countries).
  • SAGAR (Security and Growth for All in the Region): Vision for Indian Ocean region.

2. Major Relationships

  • USA: Strategic partnership (Defense agreements: LEMOA, COMCASA, BECA), trade ties.
  • Russia: Historical partner, defense cooperation (BrahMos, S-400), energy.
  • China: Complex relation — trade ties but border disputes (Galwan 2020), competition in Indo-Pacific.
  • Pakistan: Persistent issues — Kashmir, cross-border terrorism, water disputes.
  • Bangladesh: Strong ties, resolution of land boundary agreement, cooperation in power, connectivity.
  • Sri Lanka: Development projects, ethnic issue, fisheries dispute.
  • Nepal & Bhutan: Special relationships, open borders, hydropower cooperation.
  • West Asia (Gulf): Energy security, diaspora (remittances), strategic ties with UAE, Saudi Arabia, Israel.

Part 4: Important International Events & Issues (Recent)

1. Ukraine–Russia War (2022 – Present)

  • Impact on World: Global food, fuel, fertilizer crisis; inflation.
  • India’s Stand: Neutral, calls for dialogue, “Today’s Era is Not of War”. Increased oil imports from Russia.

2. Israel–Hamas War (2023 – Present)

  • India’s Position: Condemned terrorist attacks, supported two-state solution, humanitarian aid to Palestine.

3. Indo-Pacific Geopolitics

  • China’s assertiveness: South China Sea claims, Belt and Road Initiative (debt diplomacy).
  • India’s Response: QUAD collaboration, strengthening naval capacity, ASEAN centrality.

4. Climate Change & Global Agreements

  • Paris Agreement (2015): Limit global warming to well below 2°C.
  • COP26/27/28: India’s commitments — Panchamrit (Net Zero by 2070, 50% energy from renewables by 2030).
  • International Solar Alliance (ISA): India–France initiative, headquartered in Gurugram.

5. Terrorism & Global Response

  • UNSC Counter-Terrorism Committee: India chaired in 2022.
  • Financial Action Task Force (FATF): Pakistan on grey list (now removed), India advocates strict action.

6. Global Health

  • COVID-19 Pandemic: India’s “Vaccine Maitri” initiative, supply of vaccines to global south.
  • WHO reforms: India’s push for transparency and equity.

Part 8: India’s Economic Diplomacy & Key Initiatives

1. Economic Engagement Tools

  • Development Assistance:
    • Indian Technical and Economic Cooperation (ITEC): Capacity building in developing countries.
    • Lines of Credit (LOCs): Through Exim Bank for infrastructure projects abroad.
    • Aid to Neighbors: Bhutan (biggest recipient), Afghanistan, Nepal, Bangladesh.
  • Trade Agreements:
    • Free Trade Agreements (FTAs): With ASEAN, Japan (CEPA), South Korea, UAE, Australia (2022), EFTA (2024).
    • Bilateral Investment Treaties (BITs): Protection for foreign investors.

2. Key Connectivity Projects

  • International North-South Transport Corridor (INSTC): India–Iran–Russia multimodal corridor (reduces time/cost vs Suez route).
  • Chabahar Port (Iran): Strategic access to Afghanistan and Central Asia, bypassing Pakistan.
  • Kaladan Multi-Modal Transit Transport Project: India–Myanmar connectivity to Northeast India.
  • Asia–Africa Growth Corridor (AAGC): Joint India–Japan initiative for sustainable development in Africa.

Part 9: Security & Strategic Issues

1. Nuclear Policy & Doctrine

  • No First Use (NFU): Cornerstone of India’s nuclear policy since 1999.
  • Nuclear Suppliers Group (NSG): India seeks membership but faces opposition from China.
  • Missile Technology Control Regime (MTCR): India joined in 2016.

2. Maritime Security

  • Indian Ocean Region (IOR): Critical for trade and energy security.
  • Indian Navy’s Mission-Based Deployments: Presence in key choke points (Strait of Hormuz, Malacca, etc.).
  • Information Fusion Centre – Indian Ocean Region (IFC-IOR): In Gurugram for maritime domain awareness.
  • Piracy & Houthi Threats: India’s naval deployments in Gulf of Aden, Arabian Sea.

3. Border Disputes

  • China: Line of Actual Control (LAC) — Ongoing talks, confidence-building measures.
    • Major flashpoints: Doklam (2017), Galwan Valley (2020), Eastern Ladakh standoff.
    • Confidence Building Measures (CBMs): WMCC (Working Mechanism for Consultation & Coordination), border personnel meetings.
  • Pakistan: Line of Control (LoC) in Jammu & Kashmir, Sir Creek, Siachen Glacier.
  • Other neighbors: Maritime boundary with Bangladesh settled (2014), Kalapani dispute with Nepal.

4. Defense Diplomacy

  • Major Defense Partners: USA, Russia, France, Israel.
  • Joint Exercises:
    • Malabar (Naval — Quad countries)
    • Yudh Abhyas (Army — India–USA)
    • Garuda (Air Force — India–France)
    • Indra (Tri-service — India–Russia)
  • Defense Exports: To Philippines (BrahMos), Armenia, Southeast Asian nations.

Part 11: Regional Groupings in Detail

2. Indian Ocean Rim Association (IORA)

  • Members: 23 countries including India, Australia, South Africa, UAE.
  • Priority areas: Maritime safety, trade facilitation, fisheries management.

3. Organization of Islamic Cooperation (OIC)

  • India’s relation: Not a member but invited as guest of honor (2019), tensions over Kashmir.
